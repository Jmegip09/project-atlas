# Success Metrics

## Purpose

This document defines how Project Atlas measures operational success.

Success metrics provide objective measurements that evaluate business performance, operational efficiency, data quality, and overall system effectiveness. These metrics guide executive decision-making and validate whether business objectives are being achieved.

---

# Executive Metrics

## Inventory Accuracy

**Definition**

Measures how closely recorded inventory matches the actual physical inventory.

**Target**

- Greater than or equal to 98%

**Business Value**

- Improves inventory reliability
- Reduces operational disruptions
- Supports accurate reporting

---

## Inventory Value

**Definition**

Total monetary value of inventory across all warehouse locations.

**Business Value**

- Supports financial planning
- Identifies excess inventory
- Assists procurement decisions

---

## Stockout Rate

**Definition**

Percentage of products unavailable when operational demand exists.

**Target**

- Less than 2%

**Business Value**

- Improves operational continuity
- Reduces production delays
- Increases customer satisfaction

---

# Procurement Metrics

## Supplier On-Time Delivery

**Definition**

Percentage of supplier shipments received on or before the expected delivery date.

**Target**

- Greater than or equal to 95%

**Business Value**

- Evaluates supplier reliability
- Supports supplier performance reviews
- Reduces receiving delays

---

## Purchase Order Cycle Time

**Definition**

Average number of days between purchase order creation and inventory receipt.

**Business Value**

- Measures procurement efficiency
- Identifies process bottlenecks
- Improves planning accuracy

---

# Warehouse Metrics

## Receiving Cycle Time

**Definition**

Average time required to receive, inspect, and store incoming inventory.

**Business Value**

- Improves warehouse efficiency
- Reduces receiving bottlenecks
- Accelerates inventory availability

---

## Inventory Adjustment Rate

**Definition**

Frequency of inventory adjustments compared to total inventory transactions.

**Target**

- As low as reasonably possible

**Business Value**

- Indicates inventory accuracy
- Highlights operational issues
- Supports continuous improvement

---

## Warehouse Utilization

**Definition**

Percentage of available warehouse storage capacity currently in use.

**Business Value**

- Optimizes storage allocation
- Supports expansion planning
- Balances inventory across facilities

---

# Inventory Metrics

## Inventory Turnover

**Definition**

Measures how frequently inventory is consumed and replenished over a defined period.

**Business Value**

- Identifies slow-moving inventory
- Optimizes purchasing decisions
- Reduces excess stock

---

## Safety Stock Violations

**Definition**

Number of products with inventory below the established safety stock level.

**Business Value**

- Prevents stockouts
- Supports proactive replenishment
- Improves service levels

---

# Data Quality Metrics

## Data Completeness

Measures the percentage of required fields populated within operational datasets.

---

## Referential Integrity

Measures the percentage of records that maintain valid foreign key relationships.

---

## Duplicate Record Rate

Measures the number of duplicate records identified during data validation processes.

---

## ETL Success Rate

Measures the percentage of successful ETL executions without validation failures.

**Target**

- 100% successful pipeline execution

---

# Dashboard Success

Project Atlas dashboards should enable stakeholders to:

- Monitor operational performance in real time
- Identify inventory risks quickly
- Evaluate supplier performance
- Support data-driven decision-making
- Reduce manual reporting effort
