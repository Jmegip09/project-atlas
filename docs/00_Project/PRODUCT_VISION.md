# Product Vision

## Vision Statement

Project Atlas is an enterprise-style supply chain intelligence platform designed to help a fictional multi-warehouse distribution company improve inventory accuracy, supplier visibility, warehouse performance, and executive reporting.

Atlas evolves my CS-499 Inventory Management System capstone into a broader data engineering and analytics platform that connects operational data, business requirements, SQL, Python ETL, Power BI dashboards, and future cloud/data warehouse architecture.

## Company Context

Atlas Distribution Company is a fictional national distributor of industrial parts, maintenance supplies, safety equipment, tools, electrical components, warehouse consumables, and operational materials.

The company operates multiple regional distribution centers and manages thousands of SKUs, hundreds of suppliers, purchase orders, receiving activity, inventory transactions, and warehouse movements.

## Problem Statement

Atlas Distribution Company has grown, but its reporting processes have not scaled with the business.

Current challenges include:

- Inventory discrepancies discovered too late
- Limited visibility into stockouts and overstock
- Manual purchase order and receiving reports
- Inconsistent supplier performance tracking
- Difficulty identifying warehouse bottlenecks
- Reactive decision-making due to delayed operational reporting

## Proposed Solution

Project Atlas will simulate a centralized analytics platform that collects, organizes, validates, and reports on supply chain operations data.

The platform will include:

- A relational PostgreSQL database
- Realistic supply chain data
- SQL queries and reporting views
- Python ETL and validation scripts
- Data quality checks
- Power BI dashboards
- Documentation for architecture, requirements, and business logic
- Future cloud and data warehouse expansion

## Target Users

- Executive Leadership
- Operations Managers
- Procurement Managers
- Inventory Control Analysts
- Warehouse Supervisors
- Finance Analysts

## Project Objectives

- Improve inventory visibility across warehouses
- Track supplier delivery performance
- Identify stockouts, overstock, and inventory discrepancies
- Monitor purchase order and receiving activity
- Support executive KPI reporting
- Demonstrate end-to-end data engineering and analytics skills

## Success Criteria

Project Atlas will be successful when it can:

- Store realistic supply chain data in a normalized relational database
- Calculate key operational KPIs using SQL
- Detect common data quality issues
- Support Power BI dashboards for multiple business users
- Explain the business value behind each technical component
- Show a clear evolution from academic capstone to enterprise-style analytics platform

## Out of Scope for Version 1

Version 1 will not include:

- Real company data
- Proprietary of company's processes
- Live production APIs
- Machine learning forecasting
- Mobile app integration
- Full cloud automation
- Snowflake/dbt implementation

These may be considered in future versions.

## Future Vision

Future versions of Atlas may include:

- AWS-hosted PostgreSQL database
- Scheduled ETL workflows
- Snowflake data warehouse migration
- dbt transformations
- Dimensional modeling
- CI/CD with GitHub Actions
- Dockerized development environment
- API integration
- Advanced inventory forecasting
